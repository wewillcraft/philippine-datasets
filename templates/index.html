üìñ About PSGC

The Philippine Standard Geographic Code (PSGC) is a systematic classification and coding of geographic areas in the Philippines. It is updated and maintained by the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA). Each location has a unique 10-digit code that identifies its position in the administrative hierarchy.

üìö Glossary of Terms

Administrative Levels

Region
The highest administrative division in the Philippines, grouping provinces with geographical, cultural, and ethnological characteristics.
Province
The primary subdivision of regions, serving as the main unit for implementing national programs and policies.
City/Municipality
The general local government units where most government services are delivered. Cities have their own charters while municipalities are the default LGUs.
Barangay
The smallest administrative division in the Philippines and the basic unit of governance. Each barangay is headed by a Barangay Captain.
Sub-Municipality (SubMun)
Municipal districts that are administrative divisions of a municipality, typically found in geographically large municipalities.

City Classifications

HUC - Highly Urbanized City
Cities with a minimum population of 200,000 and annual income of ‚Ç±50 million. They are independent from provinces and have their own congressional districts.
ICC - Independent Component City
Component cities whose charters prohibit their residents from voting for provincial elective officials. They are independent from the province in certain aspects.
CC - Component City
Cities that are part of a province and whose residents can vote for provincial officials. They share tax revenues with the provincial government.

Special Administrative Areas

NCR - National Capital Region
Also known as Metro Manila, it's the only region without provinces. Cities and municipalities report directly to the regional government.
BARMM - Bangsamoro Autonomous Region
An autonomous region with its own government system, created through the Bangsamoro Organic Law.
CAR - Cordillera Administrative Region
An administrative region created to administer the provinces with similar cultural and geographical characteristics in the Cordillera mountain range.

PSGC Code Structure

The 10-digit PSGC code follows the format: RR-PPP-MM-BBB

  • RR - Region code (01-19)
  • PPP - Province code (000 for regions, 001-999 for provinces)
  • MM - Municipality/City code (00 for provinces, 01-99 for cities/municipalities)
  • BBB - Barangay code (000 for cities/municipalities, 001-999 for barangays)

Example: 0100100001 represents Barangay 001 in Municipality 01 of Province 001 in Region 01.
